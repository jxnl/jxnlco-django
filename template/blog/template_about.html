Hi, Jason here.

Currently, I am a student studying Scientific Computation/Applied Mathematics with some Statistics and Physics on the side. Before I transfered into the University of Waterloo's Math Degree program I studied Mathematical Physics, something I am still very passionate about. It was my love of physics and my ignorance that lead me to learn what it was that I wanted to do with my life. As it turns out, the idea of using and applying the tools from mathematics and statistics to build predictive models for anything that I care about.

Its a shame that also sounds like marketing fluff... Something about Big Data and Powerful Metrics...

I'm almost done my current work term with the Strategic Planning and Communications division at Canada's national laboratory for particle and nuclear physics TRIUMF. There my responsibility involves designing marketing material, expanding the graphic support of the lab, archiving efforts, designing and organizing conferences among other outreach events. Not only is research important but I believe that connecting the science with the public is just as important. Also, getting paid to tell people about all the cool sh*t that goes on here is really really fun. If I could, I'd love to come back again as a research assistant.
